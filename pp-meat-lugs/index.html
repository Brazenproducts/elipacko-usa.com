Why PP for Meat Processing

No odor retention

PP is non-porous at the molecular level — it does not absorb blood, fat, or protein byproducts. That means no odor carryover between product types, no biofilm formation, and no need for aggressive bleach protocols that degrade other plastics.

Temperature extremes

  • Freezer storage: copolymer PP rated to 0°F without embrittlement
  • Washdown: rated to 180°F hot water sanitization
  • No warping or deformation through freeze-thaw cycles

USDA facility requirements

PP meat lugs meet USDA facility requirements for food contact surfaces in meat processing environments. No wood, no cardboard, no absorbent surfaces.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between a meat lug and a regular container?

Meat lugs are purpose-designed for meat processing — drain plugs for blood/brine drainage, smooth radiused interior corners for complete cleanout, and copolymer PP grade for freeze-thaw cycling without cracking.

Can these be used in USDA-inspected facilities?

Yes. PP is USDA-accepted for food contact surfaces in meat processing facilities. No special certification required beyond FDA 21 CFR 177.1520 compliance.

What is the washdown temperature rating?

Elipacko meat lugs are rated to 180°F hot water sanitization — compatible with most USDA-compliant washdown protocols.

Are these the same as fish tubs?

Similar design principles. Fish tubs may have different depth-to-width ratios. Contact Elipacko if you need seafood-specific configurations.
