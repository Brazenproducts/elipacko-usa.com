Why PP for Cold Chain

What Cardboard Can't Do Below Freezing

❄️

No Compression Loss in Cold

Cardboard loses 30–50% of compression strength when exposed to moisture and cold. PP corrugated maintains rated compression strength at −40°F, wet or dry. Your stack height is predictable from cold room to dock to delivery.

💧

Zero Moisture Absorption

PP is non-absorbent. Condensation, melt water, and ice contact do not penetrate or weaken the structure. No delamination, no wet board failures, no collapsed stacks mid-transit.

🔁

Blast-Freeze Cycle Stable

Rapid temperature drop in blast-freeze tunnels does not cause brittleness, delamination, or dimensional change in PP corrugated. The same box can enter a blast freezer directly after packing and hold its shape across the full frozen supply chain.

🧼

Wash Between Cycles

After each use, pressure-wash with food-grade sanitiser. PP corrugated tolerates steam cleaning up to 212°F. No residual odor or bacterial harbourage. Ready for the next cold load within minutes of wash.

🌡️

Insulated Liner Compatible

PP corrugated boxes accept foam, reflective foil, and vacuum insulation panel liners for extended temperature hold. Liner fits inside the PP outer and is removed for washing and reuse independently.

✈️

ISPM 15 Export Exempt

No wood fiber, no fumigation requirement. PP corrugated cold chain boxes can ship to any international destination without heat-treatment or ISPM 15 documentation — a significant export compliance advantage over wood-composite packaging.

Applications

Cold Chain Industries Served

🐟 Seafood & Live Fish

Live crab, lobster, and shellfish transport with full ventilation and drainage. Blast-frozen tuna, squid, and shrimp in sealed-base formats with ice-channel base design.

🥩 Meat & Poultry

Chilled and frozen meat boxing for processing plant to distribution. Non-absorbent surface prevents blood and moisture absorption — a critical food safety requirement that wax cartons cannot reliably meet after the first use.

🥦 Fresh Produce

Cold room to retail. PP corrugated produce boxes maintain compression strength through refrigerated transport and display. Ventilated designs keep airflow consistent around leafy greens, broccoli, and other high-respiration produce.

💊 Pharmaceutical & Biotech

Temperature-controlled pharmaceutical logistics with insulated liner inserts. PP corrugated outer provides structural protection; liner maintains temperature range for cold-chain medicines, vaccines, and biological samples.

FAQs

Cold Chain Packaging Questions

Can PP corrugated boxes go directly into a blast freezer?

Yes. PP corrugated is rated to −40°F continuous. Blast-freeze cycles — rapid temperature drop to −30°F or lower — do not cause brittleness, delamination, or dimensional shift in PP corrugated. The boxes are designed to enter the blast freezer loaded and exit as part of the frozen supply chain without structural change.

What insulated liner options are compatible?

Elipacko PP corrugated cold chain boxes are designed to accept EPS foam liners, foil-bubble reflective liners, and vacuum insulation panel (VIP) inserts. The liner specification depends on your required temperature hold time and ambient temperature range — Elipacko can recommend a liner format based on your route conditions.

Are gel packs and dry ice compatible with PP corrugated?

Yes. PP corrugated is unaffected by dry ice (CO₂ sublimation, −109°F surface contact) and gel pack condensation. Neither causes surface damage, staining, or structural degradation. The non-absorbent surface resists all common cold-chain coolant media.

Can PP cold chain boxes be certified for pharmaceutical cold chain use?

PP corrugated outer packaging can support GDP-compliant pharmaceutical cold chain configurations when paired with validated insulated liners and temperature monitoring. Elipacko provides material certificates and country-of-origin documentation. Customers are responsible for qualification testing against their specific pharmaceutical cold chain specifications.
