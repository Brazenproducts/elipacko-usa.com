Why PP Corrugated

The Case Against Wax Cartons

Cost Over Time

Wax cartons are single-use. A PP corrugated box reused 10 times costs roughly the same per trip as a cheap wax carton — but the PP box holds its integrity on every cycle and creates zero contaminated waste at the destination.

Food Safety

Wax cartons absorb juice, blood, and condensation. After one use they're a contamination risk. PP is non-absorbent — wash it down with a hose and it's clean. This matters for retail customers requiring supplier food safety documentation.

Cold Chain Performance

Wax cartons lose structural integrity when wet. PP corrugated boxes maintain full compression strength at −40°F, in condensing humidity, and through pressure washing. Your stack height stays consistent from cold room to retail floor.

Regulatory Direction

Several EU and US states are moving toward banning single-use wax packaging in fresh produce supply chains. PP corrugated is already compliant. Switching now avoids a forced transition later.

FAQs

Agriculture Packaging Questions

Can PP corrugated boxes replace wax cartons in an existing cold chain?

Yes. PP corrugated boxes match or exceed the dimensional footprint of standard wax cartons. They fit existing pallet configurations, racking, and cold room layouts without any infrastructure change. The primary operational difference is the wash-and-return workflow instead of disposal at destination.

What ventilation options are available?

Elipacko offers side-panel ventilation (linear slots), end-panel ventilation, and full-perimeter ventilation depending on the crop. Ventilation area as a percentage of wall surface is specified at the quoting stage — typically 15–25% for most produce applications.

Are custom dimensions and print available?

Yes. Custom dimensions, wall thickness (2–12mm), and up to 4-color custom print are available. Elipacko provides free CAD design and a physical prototype sample before production commitment. Minimum order quantities depend on whether existing tooling fits your spec.

How do PP boxes perform in blast-freeze conditions?

Elipacko PP corrugated is rated to −40°F continuous. Blast-freeze cycles (rapid temperature drop) do not cause delamination, brittleness, or dimensional shift. The boxes are suitable for fresh-to-frozen processing lines where the same box enters a blast freezer after packing.

What is the lead time and minimum order?

One 40HQ container takes 3 days to produce. Sea freight transit to US West Coast ports is typically 14–21 days. Minimum order is typically one 40HQ container. Elipacko will confirm exact MOQ and transit time at the quoting stage based on your spec and destination port.
